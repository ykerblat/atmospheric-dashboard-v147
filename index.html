Terrestrial Moisture Recycling

Welcome. This interactive platform visualizes the physical causality between forest ecosystems and downwind rainfall stability. By quantifying these invisible connections, the tool supports evidence-based planning across critical sectors—including water resource management, energy infrastructure, urban development, and disaster risk mitigation—throughout West Africa.

How to use this tool:
  1. Select a Mode: Use the panel to toggle between Sink Analysis (identifying rainfall vulnerability) and Source Analysis (identifying critical forest nodes).
  2. Tap/Hover to Model: Select any active map grid to instantly compute and visualize its atmospheric moisture pathways.
  3. Analyze Flows: Observe the moisture pathways. In Sink Analysis, particles flow inward. In Source Analysis, particles flow outward.
    Note: Animated particles are a computer-generated visual aid to illustrate atmospheric connections and do not represent measured wind trajectories.
  4. Click to Lock/Unlock: Click on a grid to lock the view and auto-frame its ecosystem footprint. Click the exact same grid or anywhere else on the map to unlock.

Work in Progress (Pilot Status):
Please note that the analytical models driving this visualization are subject to ongoing refinement. Data paths are reconstructed via centroid interpolation using Lagrangian moisture tracking models and CHIRPS datasets.
