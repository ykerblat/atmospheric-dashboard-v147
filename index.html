Atmospheric Causality & Invisible Water

Welcome. This interactive platform visualizes the physical causality between forest ecosystems and downwind rainfall stability. By quantifying these invisible climate teleconnections, the tool supports evidence-based planning across critical sectors—including water resource management, energy infrastructure, urban development, and disaster risk mitigation—throughout West Africa.

How to use this tool:
  1. Select a Mode: Use the left panel to toggle between Sink Analysis (identifying rainfall vulnerability) and Source Analysis (identifying critical forest nodes).
  2. Hover to Model: Move your cursor over any active map grid to instantly compute and visualize its atmospheric moisture pathways.
  3. Analyze Flows: Observe the animated moisture particles. In Sink Analysis, particles flow inward (acting as a moisture vacuum). In Source Analysis, particles flow outward (acting as a moisture generator).
  4. Click to Lock/Unlock: Click on a grid to lock the view and auto-frame its ecosystem footprint. Click the exact same grid again to easily unlock.

Work in Progress (Pilot Status):
Please note that the analytical models driving this visualization are subject to ongoing refinement. Data paths are reconstructed via centroid interpolation using Lagrangian moisture tracking models and CHIRPS datasets.

Eco-Climatic Intelligence

This is a Work-in-Progress Pilot led by the World Bank. The platform visualizes the physical causality between forest cover ecosystems and downwind rainfall stability.

Scope: To quantify the value of "Atmospheric Irrigation" services and identify critical forest nodes whose degradation threatens agriculture, urban water, and energy infrastructure.

Methodology: Built on Lagrangian moisture tracking models, CHIRPS precipitation delta datasets, and Global Forest Watch integrity indices.
